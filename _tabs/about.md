# About

## Open-Source Developer & Data Standards Architect

I'm Sam Hume. I run **Hume Data Labs**, an independent practice where I build
open-source software that helps implement clinical research and healthcare data
standards, and *Munging Metadata* is where I write about that work: open-source
software, data exchange standards, data engineering, clinical research
informatics, and whatever technical problem currently has my attention.

Most of what I write grows out of code I actually ship. I build and maintain
[odmlib](https://github.com/swhume/odmlib), an open-source Python library for
CDISC ODM and the ODM-based standards. A theme runs through most of it,
**solutions over specifications**: standards earn their keep when they're
executable, not when they sit as static documents. Where I can, I post papers
here first, before they appear elsewhere.

I've spent 30+ years in the biopharmaceutical industry in senior technology
roles, including more than a decade as CDISC's VP of Data Science, and I've
given 200+ conference presentations along the way - you'd think I'd have 
the hang of it by now. Today CDISC is a client of my practice: I serve as a 
Principal Consultant co-leading the 360i Data Definition Engine and the Data 
Exchange Standards teams, and I remain an active PHUSE contributor. What I 
enjoy most is that this working in the open, alongside developers and modelers 
around the world who care about the same problems.

For the full professional history, find me on [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/sam-hume-dsc).
